Chapter 1.

Shawn woke up drenched in sweat, wearing yesterday’s, or the day before yesterday’s, clothes. The air conditioning in the basement broke again. He’s been living in the basement of his mom’s house since he was 17, when he dropped out of high school and got his GED. He thrived in the classes he liked but didn’t have enough ambition to push through the ones he disliked. His thoughts about how hot and uncomfortable he was were cut short when he heard his mom yell from the upstairs that his Uber was outside. He had totally forgotten about his interview…

Once he got to the average looking apartment building, he got out of the Uber and groaned, “another boring cubicle job.” That was the best job he could hope for though. After all, a Bachelors in Composition and Rhetoric doesn’t get you very far anymore. Shawn’s mom made him go to college in his mid twenties in the hopes that he might be able to find a job afterwards. He trudged through the courses, barely passing most of them because he never did any of the work. The interview went like all the other ones, an awkward conversation about Shawn’s previous work experience, of which he has none. He was never able to keep a job for long since he lacked ambition and work ethic. He passed it off as not finding the right fit, but the truth was that he lacked confidence and often sabotaged anything with potential because he was afraid to fail.
