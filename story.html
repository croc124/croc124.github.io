Chapter 1.

Shawn woke up drenched in sweat, wearing yesterday’s, or the day before yesterday’s, clothes. The air conditioning in the basement broke again. He’s been living in the basement of his mom’s house since he was 17, when he dropped out of high school and got his GED. He thrived in the classes he liked but didn’t have enough ambition to push through the ones he disliked. His thoughts about how hot and uncomfortable he was were cut short when he heard his mom yell from the upstairs that his Uber was outside. He had totally forgotten about his interview…

Once he got to the average looking apartment building, he got out of the Uber and groaned, “another boring cubicle job.” That was the best job he could hope for though. After all, a Bachelors in Composition and Rhetoric doesn’t get you very far anymore. Shawn’s mom made him go to college in his mid twenties in the hopes that he might be able to find a job afterwards. He trudged through the courses, barely passing most of them because he never did any of the work. The interview went like all the other ones, an awkward conversation about Shawn’s previous work experience, of which he has none. He was never able to keep a job for long since he lacked ambition and work ethic. He passed it off as not finding the right fit, but the truth was that he lacked confidence and often sabotaged anything with potential because he was afraid to fail.

“How’d it go?” Shawn’s mom Donna inquired. “It didn’t…” He responded. He brushed past her to the door to the basement and disappeared into the darkness.
She knew something had to change. She had always called Shawn, President Karsson when he was little. She had incredibly high hopes for him then. Then it hit her! Shawn’s dirty clothes, laundry time again. But what if she nominated Shawn for president? Even if it was a long shot, it may give Shawn a boost of confidence to see how many people support him. Maybe this was just the push he needed to get him moving in the right direction.

Toby purred in Shawn’s ear. Shawn nearly launched him across the room, off his bed, and out the window. Toby was a white persian cat that Donna had gotten when she saw that Shawn was getting lonely, being in that basement almost all day everyday with only her to accompany him. It must’ve so sad for him to be alone all that time.


Shawn never really liked cats, but Toby was different. He liked Toby but he never ceased to scare him. When Shawn went upstairs, his mom started to talk to him, but being half asleep he didn’t really hear her and just replied with “mmmhmm” to everything she said. “So yes?” asked his mom,

“Sure, yeah” Shawn replied without a second thought, not realizing what he just agreed to.
He headed back downstairs with his breakfast, ready for another day of job searching, and TV.
During dinner that night, his mom started getting really political at dinner, too much for his liking.
“What political party do you side with Shawn?” his mom said,
“Uhhh, yeah I uh…” he shakes his head “yeah...” Shawn said
, “Oh... okay” she replied.
She was trying really hard to get any useful information out of him without revealing what she was trying to do, but it was quite hard because of Shawn’s lack of interaction. It’s hard to learn more about someone who won’t drive a conversation or answer sincerely.
Donna went ahead and signed Shawn up for presidency that night, since he didn’t really answer what political party he is a part of, she signed him up as an independent candidate. She mustered up what was left from Shawn’s highschool graduation money, which she still doesn’t know why he didn’t spend, and sent in the registration.

A few months went by, business as usual, still no job. One day, Shawn was awakened to the sound of his mom blowing a party horn.
“Congratulations!” “WOOO!” his mom screeches at him,
“what? Oh, jeez. quiet down.” replied Shawn,
“WOOO!” his mom continued
“MOM, MY EARS!” Shawn screamed.
“Oh, sorry, I couldn’t hear you over the sound of OUR PARTY!”, Shawn just shook his head, “party?, what for?”,
“For your presidency, dummy,” his mom said.
“MY WHAT?” he seemed surprised, “I asked you about it back in November and you said sure”,
“mom I was probably tired and just trying to get through the conversation, wait...” he started pacing, “no no no no no, this can’t be happening,
“what honey, what’s wrong?”
“don’t you have to, like, compete in debates to run for president?”
“Yeah but, you can get over that fear, you can do this, I believe in you.” “Besides, there’s no way you actually get elected “ , he acts surprised
“what mom? I thought you believed in me,” he said sarcastically, “I guess you’re right. There's no way I’ll win anyways. It’s not like I can do very well campaigning without a job.”
“Listen, Sweetheart. I know things have been challenging for you and you don’t really have any direction for your life yet. I think this could be the confident boost you need. You won’t have to be President but you will have the chance to use your degree. What do you have to lose?”

A few months later while Donna was checking the mail, she got a letter that the debates were coming up, naturally she told Shawn about it. “I guess we can watch it on tv” Shawn said,
“no, I want you to go see it live”. That went nowhere, there was no way she was getting him to go. That’s the end of that, Donna thought, if she couldn’t get him to even go to a debate, there was no way anyone could even get him to compete in one. To her surprise, Shawn agreed to go to the debates. He decided it would be fun to listen to the rhetoric from all the candidates.
When he showed up, a producer found him in the audience. “What are you doing?” she asked in an exasperated tone. “You were supposed to be here an hour early to do a mic check!”
“What are you talking about? How do you know who I am? “ “Everybody knows who you are, your picture is on the advertisements for tonight’s debate. “
Shawn was totally shocked. He tried to dissuade her from leading him to the stage but she persisted and before he knew it, he was standing behind a podium alongside all of the other candidates. His shaky voice was a clear display of how nervous he was. As the night came to a close, he knew he was not in the running with all of these qualified, polished politicians.
Months went by and Shawn did nothing to further his campaign. Why would he? He was clearly out of the running.

One night, he, Donna, and Toby were lounging on the upstairs couch watching a TV when a breaking news announcement came on. A newscaster appeared and announced in a very solemn voice, “Ladies and gentlemen, we have just learned that there has been an attack on the Capital. The restaurant where the candidates running for President were having a late dinner, has caught fire and we are being told there are no survivors. “, “Wait, this just arrived on my desk. There were two candidates who were not in attendance. Shawn Karsson is still alive, and…” the news anchor leans over and whispers to someone, he quietly mumbles “is this allowed?” the anchor straightens up and says “and we have a cat named.. Toby” Shawn's mom gasps, she forgot she entered their cat into the election as a joke, she didn’t even submit the money for the submission. The news anchor continues, “We are being told that they are being contacted at this moment to be brought in.” Donna and Shawn were in complete shock. Surely, a cat couldn’t be president, could it? Donna had to do something, if Toby won, Shawn would be devastated and humiliated in front of the entire world. She has to sabotage Toby’s campaign.

FIN.
